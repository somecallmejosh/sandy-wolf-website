Discernment Counseling

Discernment Counseling is a new way of helping a specific type of couple that is usually unresponsive to traditional couples therapy.

Are you or your spouse considering divorce?

  • Have prior attempts at saving your marriage failed to make significant, lasting changes for the better?
  • Do you feel as though you are on a roller coaster of hope and despair regarding your marriage?
  • Are you worried that you could find yourself in the same position with future relationships if you did decide to leave your current one?

If so, Discernment Counseling could be your way off of that chaotic roller coaster and onto a path of clarity and confidence.

Discernment Counseling is a new way of helping a specific type of couple that is usually unresponsive to traditional couples therapy--the "mixed agenda" couple, so named because each spouse has a different starting and ending point in mind. Discernment Counseling is a chance to slow down, take a breath, and look at your options for your marriage.

Is Discernment Counseling right for me?

Discernment Counseling is best for:

  • couples where one spouse feels clear that they want to save the marriage but the other spouse does not.
  • Couples for whom traditional therapies have not worked in the past.
  • Couples who feel dangerously stuck: staying feels unbearable, leaving impossible given the financial, emotional and logistical factors at stake.

How is Discernment Counseling different from other couples' therapies?

Discernment Counseling is different in the following ways:

  • Length: short term (1-5 sessions) vs. open ended
  • Structure: the couple comes in together, but sessions are split into individual time with the counselor vs. sessions together
  • Focus: to deepen the couple's understanding of what has happened to their marriage and each spouse's own contributions to it vs. build skills, decrease conflict and improve the marriage
  • Goal: not to save the marriage, but to see if it can be saved
